Thai Green Curry with Chicken

An aromatic and flavorful Thai classic with tender chicken, vegetables, and coconut milk in a fragrant green curry sauce.

Servings: 4
Prep Time: 20 minutes
Cook Time: 25 minutes
Total Time: 45 minutes
Difficulty: Medium
Cuisine: Thai
Dietary Tags: Gluten-Free, Dairy-Free

Ingredients:

Curry Paste & Base:
- 3 tablespoons Thai green curry paste
- 1 can (14 oz) coconut milk
- 1 cup chicken stock
- 2 tablespoons fish sauce
- 1 tablespoon palm sugar or brown sugar
- 4 kaffir lime leaves

Protein & Vegetables:
- 1.5 pounds chicken breast, sliced into bite-sized pieces
- 1 cup bamboo shoots, sliced
- 1 red bell pepper, sliced
- 1 cup Thai eggplant, quartered (or regular eggplant)
- 1 cup green beans, trimmed and halved
- 1/2 cup Thai basil leaves

Aromatics:
- 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
- 3 cloves garlic, minced
- 1 inch galangal or ginger, sliced

For Serving:
- Jasmine rice
- Fresh cilantro
- Lime wedges
- Sliced red chilies (optional)

Instructions:

1. Prepare all ingredients by slicing the chicken, chopping vegetables, and measuring out the curry paste and liquids. Having everything ready makes cooking easier.

2. Heat vegetable oil in a large wok or deep skillet over medium-high heat. Add the garlic and galangal, stir-frying for 30 seconds until fragrant.

3. Add the green curry paste to the wok and cook for 2-3 minutes, stirring constantly until it becomes aromatic and the oil separates.

4. Pour in half of the coconut milk and stir well to combine with the curry paste. Let it simmer for 2 minutes.

5. Add the chicken pieces and cook for 5-6 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the chicken is mostly cooked through.

6. Add the chicken stock, remaining coconut milk, fish sauce, and palm sugar. Stir well and bring to a gentle simmer.

7. Add the kaffir lime leaves, bamboo shoots, and Thai eggplant. Simmer for 8-10 minutes until the eggplant is tender.

8. Add the bell pepper and green beans, cooking for another 3-4 minutes until the vegetables are tender-crisp.

9. Remove from heat and stir in the Thai basil leaves. Let them wilt in the residual heat.

10. Taste and adjust seasoning with more fish sauce for saltiness, sugar for sweetness, or curry paste for heat.

11. Serve immediately over steaming jasmine rice, garnished with fresh cilantro, lime wedges, and sliced chilies if desired.

Tips:
- Don't skip the kaffir lime leaves - they add authentic flavor
- For a vegetarian version, substitute tofu and use soy sauce instead of fish sauce
- Adjust the curry paste amount based on your heat preference
- The curry tastes even better the next day as flavors meld
