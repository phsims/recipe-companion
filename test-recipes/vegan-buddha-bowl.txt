Rainbow Buddha Bowl

A nutritious and colorful vegan bowl packed with roasted vegetables, quinoa, chickpeas, and a creamy tahini dressing.

Servings: 4
Prep Time: 20 minutes
Cook Time: 30 minutes
Difficulty: Easy
Cuisine: Modern Fusion
Dietary Tags: Vegan, Gluten-Free, Dairy-Free, High-Protein

Ingredients:

Base:
- 1.5 cups quinoa, uncooked
- 3 cups vegetable broth

Roasted Vegetables:
- 2 medium sweet potatoes, cubed
- 1 head broccoli, cut into florets
- 1 can (15 oz) chickpeas, drained and patted dry
- 2 tablespoons olive oil
- 1 teaspoon smoked paprika
- 1 teaspoon garlic powder
- Salt and pepper to taste

Fresh Components:
- 2 cups kale, massaged and chopped
- 1 ripe avocado, sliced
- 1 cup shredded purple cabbage
- 1 cup cherry tomatoes, halved
- 1/4 cup pumpkin seeds

Tahini Dressing:
- 1/3 cup tahini
- 3 tablespoons lemon juice
- 2 tablespoons maple syrup
- 2 cloves garlic, minced
- 4-6 tablespoons water to thin
- Salt to taste

Instructions:

1. Preheat oven to 400°F (200°C). Line two baking sheets with parchment paper.

2. Rinse quinoa thoroughly under cold water. In a medium pot, bring vegetable broth to a boil, add quinoa, reduce heat to low, cover and simmer for 15 minutes until fluffy.

3. Toss sweet potato cubes with 1 tablespoon olive oil, half the paprika, garlic powder, salt and pepper. Spread on one baking sheet.

4. On the second sheet, toss broccoli florets and chickpeas with remaining olive oil and seasonings. Make sure chickpeas are very dry for maximum crispiness.

5. Roast both sheets for 25-30 minutes, flipping halfway through, until vegetables are tender and chickpeas are crispy and golden.

6. While vegetables roast, massage kale with a pinch of salt and a drizzle of olive oil for 2-3 minutes until softened and dark green.

7. Make the tahini dressing by whisking together tahini, lemon juice, maple syrup, and minced garlic. Add water gradually until you reach a pourable consistency. Season with salt.

8. Once quinoa is done, fluff it with a fork and divide among four bowls.

9. Arrange the roasted sweet potatoes, broccoli, crispy chickpeas, massaged kale, purple cabbage, avocado slices, and cherry tomatoes in sections around each bowl.

10. Drizzle generously with tahini dressing and sprinkle with pumpkin seeds.

11. Serve immediately while vegetables are warm. Extra dressing can be stored in the refrigerator for up to 5 days.

Tips:
- Meal prep friendly - components can be prepared ahead and assembled when ready
- Swap vegetables based on season or preference
- Add extra protein with tofu or tempeh if desired
- The massaged kale technique makes it more tender and digestible
- Leftover dressing is great on salads or as a veggie dip
